1. Introduction

Welcome to Unisaver. We are committed to protecting your privacy and being transparent about how we handle information. Unisaver is designed as a privacy-focused media management application that helps you save, organize, and access content for offline viewing.

Our Philosophy

Privacy is fundamental to how we built Unisaver. Unlike many applications, we designed Unisaver to operate primarily on your device, with minimal data collection. We believe you should have complete control over your media and personal information.

Our Core Principle: Your data stays on your device. We don't collect, store, or have access to your downloaded media, viewing habits, or personal usage patterns. Your privacy is not a feature—it's our foundation.

What This Policy Covers

This privacy policy describes our practices regarding information handling when you use the Unisaver application. It explains:

  • What information stays on your device
  • What limited information we may receive
  • How we protect your privacy
  • Your rights and control over your data
  • How third-party services operate within the app

Your Consent

By downloading, installing, or using Unisaver, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this privacy policy and agree to its terms. If you do not agree with any part of this policy, please discontinue use of the application.

2. Information Storage and Collection

Unisaver operates on a local-storage model, meaning nearly all of your data remains on your device. Here's a comprehensive breakdown of what information is involved and where it's stored:

2.1 Local Device Storage (Not Accessible to Us)

All of the following information is stored exclusively within your device's application sandbox and is never transmitted to our servers:

Downloaded Media Files

  • All media content you save through Unisaver remains on your device
  • File formats may include videos, images, audio files, and documents
  • File metadata such as:
    • File name, size, and format
    • Resolution and duration (for video/audio)
    • Original source URL or reference (stored locally only for your reference)
    • Download date and time stamps
    • File location within your device storage

Organizational Data

  • Custom folders, categories, and collections you create
  • Tags and labels you assign to files
  • Sort preferences (by name, date, size, type)
  • Viewing history within the app
  • Favorites and bookmarks
  • Playlists or grouped content arrangements
  • Vault organization and security settings

Application Settings and Preferences

  • Theme selection (dark mode, light mode, custom themes)
  • Language preference for the app interface
  • Download quality settings (resolution, compression preferences)
  • Storage location preferences
  • Notification settings and preferences
  • Auto-download configurations
  • Battery-saving mode preferences
  • Network usage settings (WiFi-only downloads, cellular data limits)
  • Privacy settings (vault PIN, biometric authentication)
  • Browser settings (ad-blocking, pop-up blocking)

Browser and Navigation Data

  • Browsing history within the app's browser (stored locally only)
  • Bookmarks and saved sites
  • Website cookies (stored in app sandbox, isolated from other apps)
  • Cached website data for faster loading
  • Tab management data

What This Means for Your Privacy: Because all of this data is stored locally in your device's secure application sandbox:

  • We cannot access, view, or monitor your downloaded content
  • Your media library is completely private
  • No information about what you download is transmitted to us
  • Your organizational structure and preferences remain on your device only
  • We have no knowledge of your browsing history or viewing habits
  • Your vault contents are encrypted and inaccessible to anyone but you

5. Data Security and Protection

Protecting your information is a priority. While most of your data is stored locally on your device, we take security seriously for any data that is handled.

5.1 Local Data Security

Your Device's Protection

The security of your locally stored content depends primarily on your device's security features:

  • Operating System Security: Regular OS updates from Apple or Google
  • App Sandboxing: Isolates Unisaver's data from other apps
  • File System Permissions: Prevents unauthorized access
  • Secure Storage: Provided by your device manufacturer
  • Vault Encryption: Content in the vault is encrypted with industry-standard encryption

What You Can Do

We recommend taking these steps to protect your locally stored content:

  • Enable your device's screen lock (PIN, password, pattern, or biometric)
  • Keep your device's operating system up to date
  • Only download Unisaver from official app stores (Google Play, Apple App Store)
  • Be cautious about granting device access to other apps
  • Regularly back up important content to secure locations
  • Use device encryption if available
  • Enable vault protection within Unisaver for sensitive content
  • Use strong PIN codes or biometric authentication for the vault

Important Limitations

  • If your device is lost, stolen, or accessed by unauthorized persons, your locally stored content may be accessible to them
  • Unisaver cannot remotely lock or erase your content
  • Jailbroken or rooted devices may have compromised security
  • We cannot recover vault content if you forget your PIN or lose access

5.2 Data Transmission Security

For any data that is transmitted (such as support emails or anonymous error reports):

Encryption

  • Communications with our support team use standard email encryption
  • Error reports are transmitted over secure HTTPS connections when possible
  • Our website uses HTTPS encryption
  • API calls for download functionality use encrypted connections

Access Controls

  • Support communications are accessible only to authorized support staff
  • We implement role-based access controls
  • We limit data access to only what is necessary for each function
  • Regular security audits of our systems

5.3 Third-Party Security

Advertising Networks

Advertising partners have their own security measures in place. We work with reputable advertising networks that maintain industry-standard security practices, but we cannot guarantee the security of their systems.

App Store Platforms

Your download and installation of Unisaver through app stores are protected by those platforms' security measures (Google Play Protect, Apple App Review, etc.).

5.4 Data Breach Notification

Given that we do not collect or store significant amounts of user data on our servers, the risk of a data breach affecting your personal information is minimal. However, if we become aware of any security incident that may affect user information:

  • We will assess the scope and impact of the incident
  • We will take immediate steps to contain and remediate the issue
  • We will notify affected users if their information may have been compromised
  • We will comply with applicable data breach notification laws
  • We will provide guidance on steps users can take to protect themselves

5.5 Security Limitations

While we implement reasonable security measures, no system can be 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of information.

7. App Tracking Transparency and Your Choices

Unisaver respects your privacy choices regarding tracking and advertising. In compliance with Apple's App Tracking Transparency framework, we provide you with clear options and control.

7.1 What is App Tracking Transparency?

App Tracking Transparency (ATT) is Apple's framework that requires apps to get user permission before tracking their data across apps or websites owned by other companies, or sharing user data with data brokers.

7.2 How We Implement ATT

When you launch Unisaver on iOS devices, you may receive a permission request that asks:

"Allow Unisaver to track your activity across other companies' apps and websites?"

This permission specifically controls whether we can:

  • Access your device's advertising identifier (IDFA)
  • Use this identifier to deliver personalized advertising
  • Link data collected in our app with data collected from other companies' apps and websites for advertising purposes

7.3 Your Choices and Their Effects

If You Select "Allow":

  • You may see more relevant advertisements based on your interests
  • Advertising networks can personalize ads based on your activity across apps and websites
  • Advertising effectiveness may be measured across apps

If You Select "Ask App Not to Track":

  • We will not access your device's advertising identifier for tracking purposes
  • We will not share your information with advertisers for cross-app or cross-site tracking
  • You will still see advertisements, but they will be less personalized
  • All other app functionality remains fully available

7.4 Changing Your Mind

You can change your tracking preferences at any time:

  • iOS System Settings: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → Find and toggle Unisaver
  • Within the App: Settings → Privacy → Ad Personalization

7.5 Additional Controls

Beyond ATT, you have additional controls over your privacy:

Limit Ad Tracking (System-level)

iOS devices offer additional controls to limit advertising personalization:

  • Settings → Privacy & Security → Apple Advertising → Toggle "Personalized Ads"

Reset Advertising Identifier

You can reset your device's advertising identifier to clear advertising profiles:

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → Reset Advertising Identifier
  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads → Reset advertising ID

Premium Subscription

  • Upgrade to Unisaver Premium for a completely ad-free experience
  • No ads means no advertising tracking at all
